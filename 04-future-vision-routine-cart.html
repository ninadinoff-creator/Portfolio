Designing the Next Chapter: Routine & Cart

The cart optimization work revealed something bigger: the cart wasn't just under-optimized — it was structurally overloaded. This case study covers the vision and in-progress redesign that separates personalization from the transaction — unlocking both.

The Insight

All of this was pointing somewhere.

Every experiment in the cart surfaced the same structural issue: the cart was trying to introduce personalization, explain pricing, and close the sale — all at once. It couldn't do any of those things well because it was doing all of them.

The new Routine page gives personalization the space it deserves, while the cart becomes a clean transaction layer. Separating them also unlocks a cart that can handle multiple product categories, setting the foundation for Prose's next phase of growth.

Key Evidence

Cart experiments consistently showed that surfacing personalization details (ingredients, formulation rationale) lifted engagement and conversion. But those elements competed with pricing and checkout for space and attention. The solution wasn't better optimization — it was a new architecture.

The Vision

A new routine and cart.

The redesign introduces a dedicated Routine page between consultation results and the cart. This creates space for personalization, education, and decision-making before users ever see a checkout button.

Step 1

Step 4
Cart
Focused on the transaction. Edit quantities, manage subscriptions, proceed to checkout.
What Changes

Separation creates clarity.

The Routine page becomes the "reveal" moment — where users meet their custom formulas for the first time. Each product shows the user's name, a full ingredient breakdown, efficacy claims, and a quick-view PDP. Users build understanding and confidence before they're asked to commit.

The cart, freed from doing the work of personalization, becomes a proper e-commerce cart: clear pricing, easy editing, straightforward checkout. It can also now support multiple product categories (hair + skin) in a way the old cart never could.

Unlocking Growth

As Prose expands into skincare and beyond, the cart must handle multi-category orders. The current architecture can't do this gracefully. The Routine + Cart separation is as much about future scalability as it is about current UX.

The Full Funnel

The full funnel, cohesive at last.

With the Routine and Cart redesign, the acquisition funnel finally tells one continuous story: from the homepage where you enter your name, through the consultation that builds your profile, into a Routine page that reveals your personalized formulas, and a cart that makes buying simple.

Every piece of work over the past 18 months — the homepage redesign, the consultation experiments, the cart optimization phases — built the evidence and earned the confidence to make this happen.
